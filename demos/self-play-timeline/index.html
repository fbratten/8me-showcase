About Self-Play

Self-play uses two roles -- Proposer and Critic -- running iteratively. The proposer generates a design, the critic scores and identifies gaps, and the proposer revises.

Convergence is reached when the score improvement drops below a threshold (here: less than 1 point). This prevents oscillation -- a key risk in self-play systems (Lab 11).
